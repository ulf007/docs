## Start

- Voice AI companies have a structural knowledge gap, and it gets worse as they move upmarket.
- Engineering focus is voice quality, conversation flow, and automation, not knowledge infrastructure.
- Result: agents are only as smart as five manually uploaded files (at telli for example).
- A PDF of the product catalog. A policy doc someone exported last quarter.
- Static. No live sync. No connectors.
- Qontext fixes this without them building anything. That is where partnerships start.

## Partnership landscape

Five categories worth knowing about:

- **Vertical AI tools:** voice AI, conversational AI, AI agents.
  This is where I would start. Context quality is immediately visible in their product. Bad context means bad calls. The pain is measurable.
- **Workflow and automation platforms:** n8n, Make, Zapier.
  Their users build AI workflows that need context. Qontext becomes a node in the pipeline. Strong category, second priority.
- **Implementation consultancies:** Merantix, PLAN D, Xmethod.
  They build AI solutions for enterprises and need a context layer they can plug in across clients. Distribution into accounts Qontext could not reach directly. Worth building relationships with 2-3 early.
- **MCP ecosystem:** MCP is the emerging standard for how AI applications connect to data. Publish a Qontext MCP server, show up in the directory, and let developers find you. Community-driven growth, not a partnership to pitch, but a position to occupy.
- **Data source platforms:** HubSpot, Notion, Google Drive.
  Co-marketing, joint docs, marketplace listings. Hard to get attention at this stage. Keep on the radar, but do not spend outreach time here yet.

Where to start: Vertical AI. Where not to start: Data source platforms.

## Why vertical AI first

Three reasons, and they compound:

1. Context quality is audible.
   Wrong answer on a live call and the customer hears it. No hiding behind a UI. Value of better context is undeniable, not a nice-to-have, but a conversion and retention problem.
2. The knowledge gap is structural, not incidental.
   Voice AI startups are building voice quality, auto-dialing, and conversation flow. Knowledge infrastructure is not their core product and not on their near-term roadmap.
3. They move fast.
   Pre-seed to Series A companies make integration decisions quickly. One lunch can turn into a technical call immediately after.

Once the first one works, the playbook transfers directly.

## Telli deep dive

### The gap

- telli operates in energy, insurance, real estate, healthcare, and financial services.
- These are complex verticals with deep, dynamic knowledge spread across multiple systems.
- Native knowledge base: five static files (PDF, DOCX, TXT, or Markdown), roughly 1,500 pages max.
- No live sync. No connectors.
- Every pricing change, policy update, or product spec shift means someone manually re-exports and re-uploads.
- I reviewed all 10 published success stories. None mention knowledge or context as a feature.
- Either they have not solved it, or they have not realized it is a selling point yet. Either way, the window is open.
- This works for simple use cases such as appointment booking and basic FAQ.
- It breaks for the verticals telli is expanding into: solar configurations, insurance policy trees, medical catalogs, and financial services compliance docs.

### Who to contact and why

Two people, in the same conversation:

- Finn, CEO.
  Understands the enterprise unlock argument. Pitch: this is what lets telli sell into the complex accounts they are already chasing.
- Seb, CTO.
  Validates whether the integration is real and whether latency holds up on live calls. He needs to be in the room so technical credibility is established from the first meeting.

Format: lunch with both, casual and exploratory, founder-level.

### The pitch

<Steps>
  <Step title="Ask and listen first">
    - Ask how customers actually manage the five-file limit.
    - Ask whether enterprise prospects have flagged context as a limitation.
    - Ask what their roadmap looks like around knowledge management.
    - The goal is to understand their internal framing before positioning anything.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Frame the opportunity">
    - Context is spread across CRMs, wikis, SharePoint, and internal databases.
    - Today it is manually distilled into five files, then re-uploaded whenever something changes.
    - As telli moves upmarket, the gap between what the agent knows and what it needs to know keeps growing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Propose integration">
    - Single API call: telli's agent queries Qontext instead of, or alongside, the native knowledge base.
    - REST API, simple auth, one retrieval endpoint.
    - Native connectors already exist for the data sources telli customers use.
    - Customer connects once, agent pulls live context, no manual curation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Offer a joint success story">
    - None of telli's current success stories lead with context.
    - Run a pilot, measure results, and co-author the first one that does.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### The phased plan

<Steps>
  <Step title="Phase 1: Technical integration, roughly one week (assuming telli engineering has bandwidth — real timeline is closer to two to three weeks depending on their sprint cycle)">
    - telli agent makes a request to the Qontext retrieval API and gets ranked context with sources.
    - Main validation is latency, because voice cannot tolerate delays.
    - telli signs off on reliability and fallback behavior.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Phase 2: Joint pilot">
    - enerix is the obvious candidate: a 130-location solar franchise with product specs and pricing varying by region and installer, spread across multiple systems.
    - Run the same telli agent on two context paths: native five-file KB vs. Qontext vault.
    - Measure answer accuracy, call resolution rate, escalation rate, and CSAT.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Phase 3: Success story and formalization">
    - Co-authored case study: telli leads narrative, Qontext provides the context angle.
    - First telli story that leads with knowledge.
    - Formalize Qontext as the recommended context layer for enterprise-tier customers.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How I would run this daily

Four components:

1. Pipeline in HubSpot.
   Seven stages: Identified, Outreach, Conversation, Technical Validation, Pilot, Active Partner, Scaling.
   Each partner record tracks integration status, pilot customer, joint content status, and partner health.
   Partner health is green/yellow/red, updated manually on a regular cadence.
   The pipeline shows where every partner is and what is blocking them.
2. Scorecard before any outreach.
   Five criteria: context gap severity, customer overlap, integration ease, partner speed, strategic signal value.
   Minimum 3.5 out of 5 to pursue. Below that, park and revisit quarterly.
   The telli exercise, researching a partner's knowledge limitations from public docs, becomes a repeatable process.
3. Outreach in three tiers.
   Warm intro: double opt-in, no pitch in the intro message.
   Direct with signal: lead with a specific observation about their product, not a generic value prop.
   Cold speculative: only if scorecard is 4 or above, three sentences maximum.
   Follow-up cadence: three touches, then park if there is no response.
4. Clear automation boundary.
   Automate follow-ups, record enrichment, scheduling, stale partner alerts, and pipeline dashboards.
   AI-assist partner research, outreach drafts, and meeting prep briefs, with human review before anything goes out.
   Never automate warm intros, pitch conversations, partner health assessment, or formalization decisions.

That is where judgment lives, and judgment cannot be templated.

## Close and scale

- Once telli works, the playbook transfers.
- Same gap analysis, same integration approach, same pilot structure across Parloa, Cognigy, and Decagon.
- The first partnership is not just a partnership. It is proof the playbook works, and proof makes the next five faster.
- The system handles 10+ partners without structural change.
- Bottleneck at scale is vault setup and maintenance per partner-sourced customer.
- Hire a solutions engineer to own vault setup, data connections, and retrieval quality for every partner-sourced customer.
